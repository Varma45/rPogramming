The 2017 season of the Indian Premier League, also known as IPL 10, is the tenth season of the IPL, a professional Twenty20 cricket league established by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in 2007. The tournament features the eight teams that played in the previous season. The 2017 season started on 5 April 2017 and is scheduled to finish on 21 May 2017, with Hyderabad hosting the opening match and the final. Sunrisers Hyderabad were the defending champions having won the 2016 edition.[1]

Format[edit]

Eight teams are contesting the season. The schedule for the tournament was published on 15 February 2017.[2] The league stage, consisting of 56 matches, will take place between 5 April and 14 May 2017. The top four teams will qualify for the play-off stage, with the final being held in Hyderabad on 21 May.[1][3]

Venues[edit]

Ten venues were selected to host the matches. The opening match and the final will be played at the Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium in Hyderabad.[2]

Personnel changes[edit]

Main article: List of 2017 Indian Premier League personnel changes

The retention lists for the season were announced in December 2016.[4] On 3 February, the BCCI announced that the player auction would be held on 20 February 2017 in Bangalore with a total of 799 players registered for it.[5] On 14 February, the IPL Desk released a list of 351 players.[6] Out of the 351 players shortlisted, 66 players were sold at the 2017 IPL Auction.[7][8][9][10]

Opening ceremonies[edit]

Unlike the previous IPL seasons each of which had a single opening ceremony, the 2017 season featured opening ceremonies at each venue before the start of the first match at the venue.[11][12] The ceremonies included performances by Amy Jackson (at Hyderabad);[13] Shalmali Kholgade and Riteish Deshmukh (at Pune);[14] Bhoomi Trivedi, Sachin-Jigar and Tiger Shroff (at Rajkot);[15] Harshdeep Kaur and Disha Patani (at Indore);[16] Benny Dayal and Kriti Sanon (at Bangalore);[17] Sushant Singh Rajput and Malaika Arora (at Mumbai);[18] Shillong Chamber Choir, Monali Thakur and Shraddha Kapoor (at Kolkata);[19] Raftaar, Yami Gautam and Guru Randhawa (at Delhi).[20]
